Great Places to Live In Michigan

Grand Rapids

Grand Rapids is a fantastic place to live. The population of Grand Rapids is booming because of health care, construction and manufacturing jobs.

In Grand Rapids there are a lot of bars, restaurants, coffee shops, and parks.

The cost of living in Grand Rapids is 12% cheaper than the national average, and housing is affordable with a median home price of $113,600.

Grand Rapids is a culturally rich city with many attractions like the Frederick Meijer Gardens, the Gerald Ford Presidential Museum, and the Grand Rapids Public Museum.

Grand Rapids is big into art. ArtPrize, an annual event in which local artists enter their artworks all around town. ArtPrize culminates in a prize and award giveaway for the works that are deemed to be the best of the best.

Another reason Grand Rapids is a great place to live is because, it has a low crime rate. However, certain parts of the city have higher than average crime rates, most residential areas and outlying areas are remarkably safe and crime isn't much of a worry.

Muskegon

Muskegon is another one of the best places to live in Michigan. It has some of the best public schools in the state along with Baker College and Muskegon College. Muskegon is also home to the biggest amusement park in the state, Michigan's Adventure.

Niche.com says, "You are never far from anything you may need. The best things about Muskegon are it's Lake Michigan Beach( best dog beach ever), and poximity to Grand Rapids. Muskegon's crime rate appears to be the national average, but this is a facade."

Downtown Muskegon is filled with arts, culture, festivals and a burgeoning restaurant along with the L.C. Walker Arena, which is home to minor league ice hockey and arena football teams.

Michigan Adventure has super fun roller coasters and a waterpark. The median home value in Muskegon is $68,800, with an average household income of $30,737. The median gross monthly rent is $697.

Ann Arbor

Ann Arbor is a great city to live in and it is considered the best to live in all of Michigan. “It is at once rural and urban, sporty and smart, outdoorsy and high-tech, counterculture and high society” -U.S. News says.

It is best known as the home of the University of Michigan. Ann Arbor has excellent public schools, an increasingly diverse job market, and low crime rates make Ann Arbor an amazing place to live.

The cost of living in Ann Arbor is slightly above the national average due to the city's high taxes and higher prices for goods and services. The median home value currently stands at about $250,000 while rent for a one-bedroom apartment will typically run you around $1100 per month.

Public transportation in Ann Arbor is fairly priced at three dollars for each bus ticket but utilities may be pricier than usual at roughly eighty five dollars per month on average for electricity use.

Ann Arbor's cost of living indices are based on a US average of 100. An amount below 100 means Ann Arbor is cheaper than the US average. A cost of living index above 100 means Ann Arbor, Michigan is more expensive.

Lansing

Lansing is one of the best places to live in Michigan. Lansing has a 2.9% unemployment rate and plenty of job opportunities. It is a great place for education and those who are seeking opportunity.

Lansing's cost of living is 17% lower than the national average, and its cost of housing is 47% lower than the national average, with a median home price of $100,115 and a median rent around $850.

The weather in Michigan is fairly moderate throughout the year, but the winter is something you'll need to prepare for if you're coming from somewhere warmer.

Some of the best things to do in Lansing is to get outside and enjoy the weather throughout the city! Visit the Lansing River Trail, a 13-mile stretch along the Grand River and Red Cedar River.

The top attractions to visit in the Lansing area: Potter Park Zoo, Michigan State Capitol, Michigan History Center, Impression 5 Science Center, R.E. Olds Transportation Museum.

Holland

Holland is a great place to live in Michigan. Holland has a thriving job market and low cost of living. Holland, MI has a population of 35,219. The median income in Holland, MI is $54,556 and the median home value is $152,944.

Holland offers small town living but is still close to urban centers with access to arts, culture and entertainment. Holland is thirty minutes from Grand Rapids, the second-largest city in the state of Michigan.

Holland also holds the lowest unemployment rate in Michigan at three percent, the city has a good job market with large employers across several industries such as manufacturing, education, health care and tourism.

Holland has plenty of outdoor and recreational activities no matter the season. The city offers access to 22 parks and trails for hiking, cross-country skiing and snowshoeing and 150 miles of separate-use bike paths to explore.
